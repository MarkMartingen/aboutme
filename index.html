About Me

Education-wise, I am rooted in mathematics. Except for a two-semester stint studying mechanical engineering, mathematics has been my true calling. In 2002, I completed a thesis on percolation theory on trees and earned a Master of Science degree at The University of Warsaw. Before that I earned a Bachelor of Science degree after submitting a diploma work that dealt with groups acting on trees through automorphisms. As machine learning algorithms (or data science, if you will) began their victory march in terms of popularity, I also got more interested in this topic. My professional activity has had two main strands. Thanks to my analytical background, I have held jobs that required data processing, creating reports and building predictive models. I worked on managerial reporting for insurance companies and for a mobile phone company. I modelled insurance tariffs and predicted sales. A few years into my career I took up project management (and people management along with it) and worked mostly on delivering upgrades to various IT systems. This included gathering requirements negotiating with stakeholders, overseeing implementation and testing. To some extent project management was fun but I quickly realized that I am someone who prefers to get his hands dirty. Afterwards I worked as a data scientist for over a year at a fintech company and later as a data analyst in the e-commerce business.

In my private life I am a married man and father of two wonderful teenagers, a caretaker of two cats, an avid gardener, a leisure sailor and a motorcycle enthusiast with a knack for repairing old motorcycles. When working on a bike, I am often under the impression that solving mechanical issues resembles solving mathematical problems. The thinking process is similar although the tools are different. Instead of applying theorems, calculations, or other mathematical entities, I reach for wrenches, pliers, drills, files and so forth. This similarity may be one reason I enjoy this hobby so much.
